  • 1.0.1 

    • Undercroft of Faded Flames
    August 15th, 2020

    The wall crumbles, from it emerged two dark robed cultists. With them, they carry away the blacksmith's son, Erwin. With the goblins granting them their blessings (and some first aid), the party rushes deeper into the ruins in pursuit of the cultists. They enter the Forgotten Undercroft. Within its depths, they face its denizens and solve obscure puzzles, as they tail their quarry. In the deepest chambers, they find the cultists hunched over Erwin with a strange alchemical contraption attached to the child. Within it, a strange red liquid. The party ambushes the pair, and before long, one of the cultists conjure a stygian boar with scarlet eyes and bloody tusks. It unleashes an ashened huff, and the party begins to battle it. In its midst they are reinforced and aided by the half-orc cleric Jorna and the hulking armoured warrior Helgar, as they take down the boar. The last cultist bolts off, attempting to collapse the tunnel behind them, but Rattington goes after them in hot pursuit.

    • Mist-Direction

    The party, newly named as the Cancer Crew, head towards Minoco. Along their way, they practice some sneaky herbology, eavesdrop on some rumours, and noisily start their music career.   • 1.0.6 

    • Misty Eyes, Sundered Hands
    January 17th, 2021

    The Cancer Crew meet Meredith, an old lady who lives in the Misty Owlwood with her two grand daughters. The two run off to fetch the party's lost horses, as the crew have breakfast with the misty eyed grandmother. Strange questions are asked, spice preferences are acquired, and horses are found. After their breakfast, the party sets off towards Minoco, in the hopes of finding the missing supply cart and fulfilling Bafir's request. As they briefly pass through the Sunlit Riverland and into the Cleaven Woodland, they meet Rani Otto, a clumsy but lively young apprentice who cautiously interrogates them. They strike a bargain with him through skillful diplomacy, after chasing him through the woods, and he leads the crew to his master, Edi Wagner. They are informed about creatures wandering through the woods in packs, bearing the mark of a wounded white hand. In it is a scarlet wound, shaped almost like an eye. Furthermore, she mentions the disappearence of the regular supply cart that usually passes the woods from Minoco. The party is then paid by Edi to investigate the strange goings on of the woods, smell the pretty and locally infrequent white dogwood tree, and then enter the deeper layer of the wood. There they find the remains of the cart, and the bodies of a horse and the rider. Confirming that it is indeed the cart they were seeking, the crew turns back towards Edi Wagner's architecturally unsound hut, but are ambushed back at the dogwood tree by shadlings. The tree is accidentally burnt, and a companion is knocked out for the count during the ensuing battle. However, through clever use of magic by Ezoreal, their adversaries are put to sleep, and then slit into an eternal one by Ellie and Chaona. One of the shadlings meets their demise, and shrieks out a distorted and jumbled song. Within it the crew swear they hear their own voices scrambled together. Everyone reconvenes at the hut. The party splits, allowing their companion, Renato to rest safely, while the rest head to Minoco to report their findings. Albert Becker is woken up too early in the morning, but grumpily recognizes the crew's successful completion of the request. As one half of the party are safely guided to an inn, the other half remains at Edi's hut. There, the importance of companionship and self-care are emphasized in a spontaneous therapy session.
